# GFO Related-Party Transaction Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards for documenting transactions involving founders, family members, affiliated entities, controlled businesses, token projects, publishing entities, education programs, contractors, advisors, and related organizations.

## 2. Related-Party Definition

A related-party transaction may include any transaction involving:

- founder
- officer
- director
- member
- family member
- affiliated company
- controlled entity
- token project
- DAO
- publishing company
- education platform
- consulting brand
- contractor with personal relationship
- investor with special rights

## 3. Required Documentation

Each related-party transaction should document:

- parties involved
- relationship
- purpose
- amount
- business reason
- authorization
- payment method
- supporting contract or invoice
- disclosure classification

## 4. Fairness Standard

Related-party transactions should be commercially reasonable, documented, and explainable to:

- investors
- auditors
- tax professionals
- courts
- partners
- advisors
- regulators, if applicable

## 5. Disclosure

Related-party transactions should be disclosed when material to investor diligence, governance review, tax review, court review, or public transparency.

## 6. Approval

Material related-party transactions should be approved in writing by the appropriate authorized person, manager, board, member, or governance body.
