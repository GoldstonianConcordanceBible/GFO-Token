## Public Disclosure Rule

A public ledger entry may describe the existence of a record without publishing the private record itself.

Public entries should be factual, dated, limited, and non-defamatory.

## Private Evidence Rule

Private records remain in the private data room unless reviewed for:

- confidentiality
- privilege
- child privacy
- sealed court status
- banking information
- tax information
- contract restrictions
- investor privacy
- family privacy
- security risk

## Faith and Stewardship Statement

GFO preserves life as a ledger because memory matters.

What is built in private should be preserved with order.

What is shown in public should be shown with wisdom.

What belongs to the children should not be lost because the adults failed to document it.
