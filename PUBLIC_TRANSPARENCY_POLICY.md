# GFO Public Transparency Policy

## 1. Purpose

The Goldston Family Office maintains this public transparency policy to provide a clear record of its governance, disclosure, documentation, and accountability practices.

This policy is intended to support public trust, investor diligence, court-ready documentation, and responsible communications.

## 2. Public-Facing Standard

All public-facing GFO materials should be written in a manner that is:

- accurate
- documented
- non-misleading
- non-custodial
- non-promissory
- clearly educational or informational when applicable

## 3. No Guaranteed Returns

GFO does not make public guarantees of profit, token appreciation, yield, investment return, or financial outcome.

Any discussion of tokens, digital assets, publishing revenue, education programs, or community-governance systems must avoid language suggesting guaranteed financial benefit.

## 4. Public Repository Limits

This repository may contain policies, templates, summaries, governance frameworks, and public-facing disclosures.

This repository shall not contain:

- confidential contracts
- private banking records
- personal family records
- tax returns
- sealed court documents
- private investor information
- private wallet keys
- sensitive personal information
- confidential commercial terms

## 5. Evidence Preservation

Public-facing documentation may reference the existence of private evidence, records, contracts, invoices, receipts, or transaction logs.

The actual underlying records shall be preserved in a secure private system.

## 6. Review Standard

Before publication, GFO-related public communications should be reviewed for:

- accuracy
- legal-risk language
- investor-risk language
- custody implications
- securities implications
- tax implications
- reputational risk
- court-readiness
