## 1. Purpose

This charter records the legacy, continuity, family stewardship, and digital-asset governance intent for GFO.

This charter is intended to preserve intent and provide a governance record. It is not a substitute for a properly executed will, trust, operating agreement, custody agreement, power of attorney, beneficiary designation, or court order.

## 2. Legacy Intent Statement

GFO is intended to operate as a transparent family-office, publishing, education, consulting, governance, and digital asset documentation framework.

The long-term intent is that GFO-related assets, records, intellectual property, publications, educational systems, governance records, and digital asset documentation are preserved for the benefit of the Goldston children, subject to applicable law, valid estate documents, tax review, court orders, entity documents, and fiduciary obligations.

## 7. Digital Asset Continuity

GFO may maintain records related to wallets, tokens, digital assets, DAOs, publishing platforms, education platforms, domain names, repositories, accounts, and other digital property.

Digital asset access should be governed by:

- formal estate documents
- fiduciary access documents
- entity operating agreements
- password manager legacy access
- platform-specific legacy tools
- multisig policies
- written fiduciary authorization

North Carolina has adopted a statutory framework for fiduciary access to digital assets under Chapter 36F, but access should still be clearly authorized in estate or fiduciary documents. ## 8. On-Chain Testamentary Record

GFO may preserve a public or private on-chain record of legacy intent, governance continuity, wallet references, hashes of estate-planning documents, hashes of reports, and archive indexes.

However, an on-chain record should not be treated as a replacement for a properly executed will, trust, beneficiary designation, court order, or entity agreement.

Recommended on-chain use:

- hash of signed documents
- timestamp of legacy charter
- public governance intent
- archive index reference
- wallet inventory reference without private keys
- monthly reporting hash
- contract packet hash
- investor disclosure hash

Do not place private keys, seed phrases, full estate documents, minor child details, confidential financial records, or sealed court records on-chain.

## 11. Required Legal Conversion

This charter should be converted into formal documents as appropriate, including:

- last will and testament
- revocable living trust
- digital asset authorization
- operating agreement amendment
- beneficiary designations
- power of attorney
- healthcare directive, if desired
- trust or custodial account documents for minor beneficiaries
- fiduciary access instructions
- multisig succession policy

North Carolina’s UTMA framework uses custodianship for property transferred to minors, but that is different from a trustee, executor, or records custodian role. Use specific legal documents to define which role Elise Goldston, Junitha Ann Goldston, or any other person is actually serving.
