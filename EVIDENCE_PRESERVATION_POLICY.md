# GFO Evidence Preservation Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards for preserving business, governance, contract, investor, publishing, digital asset, and public communications records.

## 2. Preservation Standard

GFO should preserve records in a way that supports:

- court-readiness
- investor diligence
- audit review
- tax review
- contract enforcement
- public accountability
- dispute resolution
- governance continuity

## 3. Records to Preserve

GFO should preserve:

- executed contracts
- SOWs
- invoices
- receipts
- bank records
- payment confirmations
- wallet transaction records
- board or member consents
- investor communications
- public communications
- screenshots
- email correspondence
- deliverables
- GitHub commits
- course-design materials
- publishing records
- disclosure summaries

## 4. Chain of Custody

Important records should include:

- date created
- author or source
- storage location
- file name
- hash or version number, if applicable
- summary of relevance
- access history, when available

## 5. Public Repository Records

Public repositories may preserve:

- policy documents
- templates
- public summaries
- redacted governance records
- version history
- public communication drafts

Private records should not be uploaded publicly unless reviewed and approved.

## 6. Litigation Hold

If a dispute, claim, investigation, subpoena, investor inquiry, tax review, court matter, or regulatory issue becomes reasonably foreseeable, relevant records should not be deleted, altered, overwritten, or destroyed.

## 7. Monthly Preservation Review

GFO should perform a monthly review of:

- new contracts
- new payments
- new public statements
- new digital asset activity
- new investor communications
- new governance decisions
- new deliverables
