# GFO Court-Ready Recordkeeping Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes recordkeeping practices designed to make GFO’s contracts, revenue, expenses, public communications, governance actions, and business activity understandable to a court, auditor, attorney, investor, or other reviewing party.

## 2. Recordkeeping Principles

GFO records should be:

- dated
- organized
- accurate
- complete
- preserved
- explainable
- source-backed
- separated from private personal records where appropriate

## 3. Required Business Records

GFO should preserve:

- contracts
- SOWs
- invoices
- receipts
- payment records
- bank statements
- accounting ledgers
- public communications
- investor communications
- digital asset records
- governance approvals
- publishing records
- course-design deliverables
- consulting deliverables
- correspondence logs

## 4. Court-Ready Summary Standard

For any material transaction, GFO should be able to answer:

- Who was involved?
- What was agreed?
- When was it agreed?
- What was delivered?
- When was it delivered?
- What was paid?
- How was it paid?
- Where is the proof?
- How was it classified?
- Was any disclosure required?

## 5. Public and Private Separation

Public records may include policies, templates, and redacted summaries.

Private records may include contracts, payment records, bank statements, tax documents, and confidential communications.

## 6. No Destruction of Relevant Records

If a dispute, court issue, audit, investor inquiry, regulatory inquiry, or contract claim is reasonably foreseeable, relevant records should be preserved and not destroyed.

## 7. Monthly Review

GFO should conduct a monthly review to confirm that material records have been preserved and properly classified.
