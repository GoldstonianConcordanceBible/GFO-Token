# GFO Redaction Rules

## 1. Purpose

These redaction rules govern what may and may not be published in GFO public repositories, public reports, public ledgers, investor summaries, and court-ready public exhibits.

## 2. Core Rule

Public summaries may disclose structure, chronology, governance, categories, and general purpose.

Private evidence should remain private unless reviewed and approved.

## 3. Always Redact Before Public Release

The following should be redacted before public release:

- minor child private information
- school information
- medical information
- Social Security numbers
- birth dates
- home addresses
- bank account numbers
- tax ID numbers
- private wallet keys
- seed phrases
- passwords
- private family communications
- sealed court records
- confidential contract terms
- investor-identifying information
- attorney-client privileged material
- settlement communications
- private phone numbers
- private email addresses unless already public and relevant
- signatures where unnecessary
- account login details
- private platform dashboards
- unredacted payment records
- unredacted tax records

## 4. Public Summary Allowed

The following may generally be summarized publicly:

- existence of a contract
- category of revenue
- existence of private evidence
- public-facing governance policy
- public research citation
- public article
- public GitHub commit
- public video
- public educational project
- public investor disclosure framework
- public digital asset policy
- public monthly transparency summary

## 5. Court-Ready Does Not Mean Public

Court-ready evidence may still be private, sealed, confidential, privileged, or inappropriate for public release.

A record can be preserved for court without being posted publicly.

## 6. Investor-Ready Does Not Mean Public

Investor-ready diligence may include private materials shared only under confidentiality, data-room access, attorney review, or formal diligence process.

## 8. Review Before Release

Before publishing sensitive material, review for:

- legal risk
- defamation risk
- family privacy
- child privacy
- contract confidentiality
- court restrictions
- privilege
- tax sensitivity
- investor privacy
- cybersecurity risk
