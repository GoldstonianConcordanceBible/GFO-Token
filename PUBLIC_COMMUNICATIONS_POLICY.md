# GFO Public Communications Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs public communications related to GFO, GFO Token, Goldston Family Office Blackbook, related publishing projects, digital asset research, DAO discussions, educational initiatives, and investor-facing materials.

## 2. Communication Standard

All public communications should be:

- truthful
- documented
- non-misleading
- non-promissory
- non-custodial
- clear about risks
- clear about educational or research purpose

## 3. Prohibited Language

Public communications should avoid statements that suggest:

- guaranteed profit
- guaranteed token price
- guaranteed investor return
- guaranteed liquidity
- risk-free participation
- official regulatory approval
- bank-like custody
- investment advice
- insider access
- unavoidable appreciation

## 4. Approved Framing

Preferred language includes:

- educational research initiative
- governance framework
- public transparency record
- non-custodial digital asset discussion
- family-office documentation system
- publishing and education infrastructure
- contract-management framework
- investor-readiness documentation
- public accountability framework

## 5. Social Media

Social media posts should be treated as public records.

Posts involving GFO, token discussions, investors, contracts, court-ready documentation, or governance should be preserved when material.

## 6. Review Process

Before publishing high-risk communications, review for:

- securities implications
- custody implications
- tax implications
- investor expectations
- legal claims
- factual accuracy
- reputational risk
- court-readiness

## 7. Correction Policy

If a public statement is inaccurate, incomplete, or misleading, GFO should correct the record promptly and preserve both the original statement and the correction.
