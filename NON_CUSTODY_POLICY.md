# GFO Non-Custody Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes GFO’s non-custodial posture for digital assets, wallets, tokens, DAOs, and public-facing blockchain-related initiatives.

## 2. Non-Custodial Position

GFO does not hold itself out as a custodian of user funds, investor funds, customer assets, or third-party digital assets unless a formal legal structure and regulated custody arrangement is established.

## 3. Wallet Responsibility

Participants are responsible for their own wallets, private keys, seed phrases, signatures, and blockchain interactions.

GFO should not request, store, manage, or control private keys or seed phrases.

## 4. No Exchange Function

GFO does not present itself as a securities exchange, commodities exchange, broker-dealer, swap dealer, investment adviser, bank, or money transmitter through this public repository.

## 5. Educational and Governance Use

Any token-related discussion in this repository should be treated as educational, governance-oriented, experimental, or research-based unless otherwise stated in a formal legal document.

## 6. Public Communications

Public communications should avoid language that implies:

- guaranteed profit
- guaranteed liquidity
- guaranteed appreciation
- passive income
- custodial protection
- investment advice
- official exchange listing
- regulatory approval
- risk-free participation

## 7. Security Practices

GFO-related projects should document:

- wallet permissions
- multisig standards, if used
- signer roles
- treasury policies
- transaction logs
- public communications approvals
- incident response procedures
