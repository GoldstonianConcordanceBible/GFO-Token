# GFO Contract Management Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the contract-management standards for Goldston Family Office-related consulting, publishing, course-design, educational, technology, research, digital asset, and governance projects.

## 2. Contract File Structure

Each contract or project should maintain a structured record folder containing:

- executed agreement or SOW
- proposal or scope document
- invoice
- payment record
- deliverables log
- correspondence record
- amendment history
- completion evidence
- revenue classification note
- public disclosure classification

## 3. Standard Folder Format

Recommended folder structure:

/contracts/YYYY/client-or-project-name/

Required files:

01_SOW.md  
02_Executed_Agreement.pdf  
03_Invoice.pdf  
04_Payment_Record.pdf  
05_Deliverables_Log.md  
06_Correspondence_Index.md  
07_Revenue_Classification.md  
08_Disclosure_Review.md  
09_Completion_Summary.md  

## 4. Revenue Classification

Each contract should be classified by business activity, such as:

- consulting
- course design
- educational publishing
- curriculum development
- research services
- AI implementation advisory
- supply chain advisory
- digital asset governance research
- public communications
- technology implementation

## 5. Deliverables Log

Each contract should include a deliverables log showing:

- deliverable name
- due date
- completion date
- responsible party
- delivery method
- approval status
- notes

## 6. Amendments

Any change to scope, payment, deadlines, ownership, usage rights, or deliverables should be documented in writing.

## 7. Court-Ready and Investor-Ready Recordkeeping

Each contract file should be capable of demonstrating:

- who hired GFO or the contractor
- what work was requested
- what work was delivered
- when work was completed
- how payment was made
- how revenue was classified
- whether any related-party issue exists
- whether public disclosure is required
