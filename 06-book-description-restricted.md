# Restricted Book Description and Publishing Guidelines

## I. Purpose

This document governs the metadata, back-cover language, Amazon KDP description, IngramSpark description, WorldCat metadata, website description, and promotional copy for the Goldston Family Office Blackbook and related GFO publications.

The goal is to prevent publishing copy from accidentally characterizing the book, token, DAO, or family office as a financial product, securities offering, custody service, investment service, money-transmission service, or public asset-management platform.

## II. Restricted Book Description

The Goldston Family Office Blackbook is a compliance-aware, non-custodial research playbook for service-focused token communities, decentralized governance, publishing, education, identity, and community-governance research initiatives.

Conceived by Dr. Justin Goldston as part of the broader Global Social Experiment framework and chronicled through the voice of Thothan Atlantos, the Blackbook examines how books, public scholarship, cultural access, governance signals, decentralized identity, and community participation can be organized without custody, pooled public assets, redemption guarantees, or investment promises.

The book is written for researchers, developers, policymakers, educators, community advocates, and systems architects studying the intersection of decentralized infrastructure, public goods, publishing, and non-custodial participation.

The Goldston Family Office Blackbook does not offer custody, investment management, securities, banking, brokerage, exchange services, money transmission, redemption rights, yield, or guaranteed financial return.
