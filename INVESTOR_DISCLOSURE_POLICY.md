# GFO Investor Disclosure Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a public-facing disclosure framework for investor diligence, capital-readiness, token-related discussions, governance review, and business transparency.

## 2. Investor-Ready Position

GFO seeks to maintain documentation that allows an investor, auditor, attorney, court, or strategic partner to understand:

- business model
- governance structure
- contract activity
- revenue sources
- expense categories
- token-related risks
- non-custody posture
- public communications policy
- evidence-preservation practices

## 3. No Investment Guarantee

No GFO-related public material should state or imply that investors, token holders, contributors, members, or participants are guaranteed profit, income, appreciation, yield, liquidity, or resale value.

## 4. Risk Disclosure

Investor-facing materials should disclose that digital assets, tokens, DAOs, publishing revenue, education programs, consulting revenue, and early-stage ventures involve risk.

Potential risks may include:

- regulatory risk
- market risk
- liquidity risk
- operational risk
- technology risk
- smart-contract risk
- tax risk
- governance risk
- reputational risk
- business-execution risk

## 5. Source of Funds and Use of Funds

When applicable, GFO should maintain private records showing:

- source of funds
- use of funds
- contract revenue
- operating expenses
- owner compensation
- contractor payments
- software expenses
- legal and compliance expenses
- publishing expenses
- education platform expenses

## 6. Public Versus Private Disclosure

Public materials may summarize governance, risk, and transparency practices.

Private investor materials may include more detailed financial, contractual, operational, or tax records, subject to confidentiality and legal review.

## 7. Related-Party Transactions

Any transaction involving founders, family members, affiliated entities, related companies, or controlled projects should be documented and classified.

Related-party transactions should include:

- parties involved
- relationship
- purpose
- amount
- authorization
- business reason
- supporting documents

## 8. Review Before Offering

Before any formal securities offering, token sale, investment round, SAFE, note, equity issuance, revenue-share arrangement, or investment solicitation, GFO should obtain legal review from qualified counsel.
